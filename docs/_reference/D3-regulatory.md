# **1. Executive Summary**
This deliverable maps the regulatory obligations facing the venture across two jurisdictions: the United States, where the founder operates LawOne Cloud LLC and intends to sell the U16H and U19M wrist BP smartwatches as the lead market; and Nigeria, where a parallel pharmacy and B2B channel is planned alongside US revenue.

The single most important finding emerged during D3 research: FDA Establishment Registration and Device Listing — the database entry that names the U16H and U19M among Urion's listed devices — is administrative, not authorisation. The legal authorisation to sell a Class II Noninvasive Blood Pressure Measurement System (Product Code DXN, 21 CFR 870.1130) in the United States is a 510(k) Premarket Notification clearance. After direct verification of the FDA database, the relevant clearance is K141683, which lists the U16H, U16L, U16P, U16W, U19M, U19R, U19S, and U19T as covered proprietary names alongside the Andesfit ADF-B103W and the Urion U60 series.

This means the wrist watches are covered — but the legal right to import and market them in the United States hinges on a Letter of Authorization from the 510(k) holder permitting LawOne Cloud LLC to reference K141683 as initial importer. That document is the gating item before the founder commits inventory capital.

On the Nigerian side, NAFDAC registration as a Class B medical device is required, takes four to six months, and requires a Nigerian-incorporated entity acting as authorized representative of Urion. SONCAP conformity assessment may also apply at import.

# **2. The 510(k) Finding**
## **2.1 Why This Matters**
Two FDA processes are commonly conflated and must not be:

- **Establishment Registration and Device Listing (21 CFR Part 807)** is administrative. It tells FDA who exists and what they are placing on the US market. Per FDA's own guidance, registration does not indicate FDA approval or clearance of the establishment or its products. Implying that listing equals clearance is misbranding under 21 CFR 807.39.
- **510(k) Premarket Notification clearance** is the marketing authorization for Class II devices. For wrist BP monitors (Product Code DXN, 21 CFR 870.1130), 510(k) clearance is mandatory before commercial distribution.

The project context inherited the phrase "FDA Device Listed" without distinguishing between these two facts. D3 surfaced this risk and required direct verification.
## **2.2 What the FDA Database Confirms**
Direct lookup of Urion's establishment registration (Owner Operator Number 10049394, Registration Number 3011654863) returns the following relevant entry, with current registration year 2026:

## **2.3 The Probable Regulatory Structure**
Andesfit Ltd ADF-B103W appears as the first proprietary name on K141683. Andesfit is a Hong Kong-based labeler that sells Bluetooth BP monitors on Amazon. The most probable structure is: Andesfit holds K141683 as the original 510(k) submitter, and Urion is the contract manufacturer. Over time, additional Urion model numbers (U60, U16, U19 series) have been added to the clearance as brand variants of the underlying device design.

This is a common and legitimate Chinese OEM regulatory structure used by iHealth, Andon, Wellue, and many others. It does not invalidate the clearance — but it does mean the founder cannot simply rely on Urion's authorization. The founder needs explicit permission from the 510(k) holder to reference K141683 as initial importer.

# **4. United States Regulatory Roadmap**
## **4.1 Initial Importer Registration (21 CFR 807.20)**
LawOne Cloud LLC becomes the Initial Importer — the entity that furthers the marketing of a foreign-manufactured device to the person who makes the final delivery or sale to the consumer.

Required actions:

- Register LawOne Cloud LLC in FURLS (FDA Unified Registration and Listing System) at access.fda.gov.
- File within 30 days of starting covered operations (21 CFR 807.22).
- Renew annually between October 1 and December 31 (21 CFR 807.21).
- **FY2026 fee:** $11,423, payable via Pay.gov before submitting registration.
- List each device imported, referencing K141683.
- Submit name and address of foreign manufacturer (Urion).
## **4.2 Renewal Window — Critical**
Missing the December 31 annual renewal results in: automatic suspension of registration; cancellation of device listing; CBP shipment holds at the port; possible Import Alert. There is no grace period. Calendar this aggressively. The Small Business Determination waiver may apply for initial registration only — worth checking, but plan to pay full freight.
## **4.3 US Agent for Foreign Manufacturer (21 CFR 807.40)**
Urion needs a US Agent registered with FDA. Two paths:

- Confirm Urion already has one (likely, given they have been exporting since the K160019 clearance in 2016).
- LawOne Cloud LLC could serve as US Agent, but this creates dual-role exposure. Recommendation: confirm Urion's existing US Agent first; do not take on the role unless required.
## **4.4 Labeling Compliance (21 CFR 801)**
Every device must bear a label that is truthful, not misleading, and includes:

- Name and place of business of the manufacturer, packer, or distributor (importer info).
- Adequate directions for use including statement of purpose, frequency, route, preparation.
- Warnings and contraindications.
- Unique Device Identifier (UDI) per 21 CFR 801.20.
- Statement matching the cleared Indications for Use exactly — no expansion.
## **4.5 Marketing Claims — The Critical Constraint**
This is where most Chinese OEM rebrands get into trouble. Urion's existing Chinese-market marketing for the U16H and U19M includes claims that are not cleared under K141683 and may not be cleared under any DXN-pathway 510(k). The following table separates safe claims from claims that must not appear in US marketing:

|**✓ Permitted Claims**|**✗ Prohibited Claims**|
| :- | :- |
|Measures blood pressure on the wrist using clinical oscillometric technology|AI-powered diagnosis|
|FDA-cleared blood pressure measurement (510(k) K141683)|Stroke prediction or stroke warning|
|Track blood pressure trends over time|Predicts cardiovascular events|
|Wellness features include sleep, steps, heart rate monitoring|Traditional Chinese Medicine pulse diagnosis|
|Built using ISO 13485 quality systems|Medical-grade pulse oximeter / clinical SpO2|
|Pulse rate (BPM) measurement|Diagnoses hypertension (only physicians diagnose; device measures)|

Why this matters in practice: FDA has been increasingly active against unbranded BP smartwatch sellers on Amazon through 2025–2026. Several have received warning letters and Amazon listing removals for over-claiming features beyond their 510(k). Build US marketing copy outward from the cleared Indications for Use — never inward from Urion's existing Chinese-market positioning.
## **4.6 Quality System Regulation (21 CFR 820 → QMSR effective February 2026)**
As Initial Importer, the founder is not the manufacturer, so most of 21 CFR 820 sits with Urion. But the importer has these obligations:

- Acceptance activities: inspect incoming product and document conformance (21 CFR 820.80).
- Complaint handling: receive complaints, log them, forward to manufacturer (21 CFR 820.198).
- Records: keep distribution records for 2+ years post-sale (21 CFR 820.180).

Note: in January 2024, FDA harmonized 21 CFR 820 with ISO 13485 via the new Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR), with full compliance required by February 2, 2026. Urion's existing ISO 13485 certificate (TÜV SÜD, valid to 2027) satisfies the manufacturer side. The importer needs only a basic SOP for receiving / inspection / complaint handling. Estimated cost: $2,000.
## **4.7 Medical Device Reporting (21 CFR 803)**
If a customer reports an injury, malfunction, or death potentially related to the device, the Initial Importer must report to FDA within 30 days (21 CFR 803.40). Practical setup:

- Customer service must be trained to flag any health complaint as a potential reportable event.
- Logged complaints require initial assessment within 5 days.
- File via FDA's eMDR (electronic Medical Device Reporting) system.
- Build a complaint log into the existing Supabase infrastructure from day 1.
## **4.8 UDI and GUDID**
Every device must carry a Unique Device Identifier on its label and packaging, and the device record must be in GUDID (Global Unique Device Identification Database). Urion is already in GUDID for the U16/U19 series. Confirm UDI strings are discoverable at accessgudid.nlm.nih.gov. If re-labeled under the founder's brand, the GUDID record may need updating — discuss with Urion.
## **4.9 Other US Regulatory Bodies**
- **FTC:** marketing claims must be substantiated. "Medical-grade accuracy" is fine if K141683 clinical validation supports it. "Doctor-recommended" requires actual doctors. False health claims are FTC's enforcement focus.
- **FCC:** BLE 5.x is FCC Part 15 territory. Urion's device requires FCC ID for the radio module, which must appear on the physical device label.
- **California Prop 65:** warnings if any battery / electronic chemicals exceed thresholds. Generally batteries do not, but request Urion's certificate of compliance.
- **HIPAA:** only applies if the venture becomes a Business Associate to a Covered Entity. For DTC consumer sales, not directly applicable. Strong privacy policy still required under state laws (CCPA, etc.).


# **5. Nigeria Regulatory Roadmap**
## **5.1 NAFDAC Medical Device Registration**
NAFDAC regulates medical devices under the Medical Devices Registration Regulations, 2021. A wrist BP monitor classifies as Class B (low-moderate risk).

Required documentation:

- Application letter on company letterhead.
- Certificate of Manufacture (Urion's issuing-country authorization).
- Free Sale Certificate or equivalent (Urion's CE MDR satisfies).
- ISO 13485 quality management certificate (Urion has, valid to 2027).
- Power of Attorney from Urion appointing the Nigerian entity as authorized representative.
- Product technical file (specifications, labeling, IFU in English).
- Sample for laboratory testing (one device).
- Evidence of incorporation of the importing company (CAC documents).
- NAFDAC Premises Inspection of the importer's storage facility.
- Application fee approximately ₦70,000 per product (~$50).

Timeline: officially 90–120 working days; realistic timeline is 4–6 months. Validity: 5 years from approval date.
## **5.2 Local Entity Required**
A foreign company cannot import medical devices into Nigeria. A Nigerian-registered business is required:

- **Limited Liability Company (Ltd)** registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) — recommended option.
- **Sole Proprietorship / Business Name** — simpler to register but less credible for B2B sales to pharmacies and clinics.

Cost: ~₦50,000–100,000 ($35–$70) plus lawyer fees. Timeline: 2–3 weeks. Operating in Port Harcourt is fine.

Also required: TIN (Tax Identification Number) from FIRS; VAT registration (charge 7.5% VAT to customers); office lease or utility bill for premises verification.
## **5.3 SONCAP — Standards Organization of Nigeria Conformity Assessment Programme**
Wrist BP monitors are likely subject to SONCAP requirements unless exemption applies. SONCAP requires:

- Pre-shipment Verification of Conformity at the Chinese factory by an SON-accredited testing organization (Cotecna, Intertek, Bureau Veritas, SGS) — approximately $500–$800 per shipment.
- Issued Certificate of Conformity (CoC) before customs clearance.

Action: verify with the Nigerian customs broker whether SONCAP applies to HS 9018.19 medical electrodiagnostic with NAFDAC registration in place. There are sometimes exemptions for properly registered medical devices. A 30-minute call with a Lagos-based customs broker resolves this.
## **5.4 Customs Duty and Fees**
From D2: conservative model assumes 5% ECOWAS Common External Tariff. Optimistic case is 0% (medical Band 0). Resolution: request a binding tariff classification ruling from Nigeria Customs Service for HS 9018.19. Free, takes 4–8 weeks.

Other Nigerian import charges (D2 confirmed):

- ECOWAS Trade Liberalization Scheme (ETLS) levy: 0.5% of CIF.
- Comprehensive Import Supervision Scheme (CISS): 1% of FOB.
- 7% Surcharge of duty.
- VAT: 7.5% on (CIF + duty + other charges).
## **5.5 Pharmacy Channel and PCN**
The Pharmacists Council of Nigeria (PCN) regulates pharmacy practice. Medical devices sold through pharmacies do not directly trigger PCN registration on the venture's end (it is the pharmacy's compliance), but NAFDAC-registered status is a precondition for any PCN-registered pharmacy to legally stock the product.
## **5.6 Advertising and Claims**
NAFDAC and the Advertising Practitioners Council of Nigeria (APCON) regulate health-related advertising. Claims must match registered Indications for Use. Pre-vetting required for media advertising of medical products. Same discipline as US: marketing copy must align with what is registered.
## **5.7 Data Protection (NDPR)**
Nigeria's Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) governs personal data. Health data is classified as sensitive. App-related obligations:

- Privacy policy required (in English).
- Data processing under lawful basis (consent for sensitive data).
- NDPR audit (annual, mandatory if processing more than 1,000 data subjects).
- Audit cost: ₦50,000–200,000 / year via an NDPR-licensed Data Protection Compliance Organization (DPCO).


# **6. White-Label Contract — Required Terms**
The verbal white-label agreement must be formalized in a Manufacturing & Supply Agreement before the first PO. Verbal terms with Chinese suppliers do not survive contact with regulators or disputes. Required clauses:

1. Written confirmation of which 510(k) (K141683) and CE certificate covers U16H and U19M; copies attached as annex.
1. License to use the 510(k) holder's clearance for US import — legally required for the founder to reference K141683 as Initial Importer.
1. License to apply the founder's brand label and QR code to device and packaging.
1. Manufacturer warranty terms — coverage scope, claim procedure, RMA process.
1. Lead time and MOQ for re-orders — typical Chinese OEM is 30–45 days production after PO.
1. Payment terms — typical is 30% deposit, 70% on shipment. Negotiate L/C if possible for first orders.
1. Quality conformance — incoming inspection AQL standards, defect tolerance, replacement policy.
1. Regulatory cooperation clause — Urion will provide FDA documentation if requested; cooperate on any vigilance issue.
1. Indemnity for IP and regulatory infringement — Urion warrants the device does not infringe third-party patents AND complies with claimed regulations.
1. Trademark exclusivity (geographic) — Urion cannot sell the founder's branded SKU to other parties in the US or Nigeria.
1. Right to terminate — exit cleanly if quality fails.
1. Governing law / dispute resolution — Hong Kong arbitration (HKIAC) is the standard for Sino-Western disputes.

# **9. Residual Risks**

FDA's guidance "Deciding When to Submit a 510(k) for a Change to an Existing Device" suggests changes to form factor, environment, or add-on features can sometimes trigger a new 510(k). Mitigation: have the regulatory consultant evaluate whether the smartwatch features bring the device into different intended use territory. Most likely fine if marketed as a BP device with wellness add-ons, but worth a formal opinion before launch.</p>|
